I.
Family and personal context
An HUF reflects a family unit. The composition of your immediate family shapes how the structure works for you.

What an HUF is, in brief

A Hindu Undivided Family is recognised as a separate taxable entity under the Income Tax Act, 1961. It has its own Permanent Account Number, its own bank accounts, and its own tax filings — entirely distinct from your personal records.

Forming an HUF requires a valid family unit known as a coparcenary. In practical terms, this means at least two related family members. Following the Hindu Succession (Amendment) Act, 2005, daughters and sons are equal coparceners.

Eligibility to form an HUF is governed by Indian personal law. Your advisor will confirm applicability based on your specific circumstances.

ReferenceChildren are coparceners by birth. Their presence (current or future) strengthens the long-term continuity of the HUF.
ReferenceAn extended family unit often already has an existing ancestral HUF that you may belong to. Where this is the case, joining or continuing that HUF is a common approach.
ReferenceAncestral wealth represents the most established and tax-efficient basis for an HUF. Income from such assets is naturally attributable to the HUF.
II.
Income profile and tax regime
Understanding your current tax position helps determine whether the HUF structure offers meaningful benefit and which regime would be most appropriate.

How HUF taxation works

The HUF is a separate taxpayer with its own basic exemption — ₹4 lakh under the new regime and ₹2.5 lakh under the old regime for FY 2025–26 — and its own slab structure. Investment income that legitimately belongs to the HUF is taxed within these slabs.

An important distinction: the Section 87A rebate, which allows individuals to pay zero tax on income up to ₹12 lakh under the new regime, does not extend to HUFs. The HUF's benefit comes from slab arbitrage and applicable deductions, rather than the rebate.

The HUF can choose its tax regime independently of your personal choice. The optimal regime depends on the HUF's expected income mix and the deductions it will utilise.

ReferenceSalary income is personal and remains taxed in your hands. Rental income, capital gains, business income, and interest from HUF-held assets can be taxed within the HUF.
ReferenceThe HUF may independently choose between regimes. A personalised recommendation will be provided in the summary, based on your profile.

IV.
Assets, capital, and property
An HUF requires an initial corpus, and the source of that corpus determines its tax treatment. For families with multiple properties, the HUF also offers a specific advantage.

The multiple-property benefit

Under Section 23(2) of the Income Tax Act, an individual may hold up to two residential properties as self-occupied, with the annual value treated as nil. From the third property onward, the property is treated as "deemed let out" and notional rent becomes taxable, even if the property is vacant.

Because the HUF is a separate person in the eyes of the Act, it independently qualifies for the same allowance. A family may therefore hold up to four residential properties at nil annual value — two in the individual's name and two in the HUF's. This treatment is available only to individuals and HUFs.

ReferenceGifts from non-member relatives (parents, in-laws, siblings) and ancestral assets are exempt from clubbing under Section 64(2). Personal funds transferred to your own HUF attract clubbing provisions and reduce the structure's tax efficiency.
ReferenceIndividuals may hold up to two self-occupied properties at nil annual value. Properties beyond this are treated as deemed let out, and notional rent becomes taxable.

Important considerations
An HUF involves shared family rights and a long-term commitment. These questions help ensure the structure aligns with your expectations.

ReferenceWhere personal assets are transferred to your own HUF without consideration, the income on such assets is clubbed back in your hands. Funding through gifts from relatives or ancestral sources avoids this.
